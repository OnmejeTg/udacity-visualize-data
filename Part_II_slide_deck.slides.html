Investigation Overview¶

In this exploration I want to demostrate that there is a strong negative corrolation between the BorrowersAPR and the Proser Score ie. the higher the prosper score the lower the APR. Also that the higher the loan amount the lower the borrowersAPR showing a negative corrolation between the two

Dataset Overview¶

This data set contains 113,937 loans with 81 variables on each loan, including loan amount, borrower rate (or interest rate), current loan status, borrower income, and many others. the loans were given by proser loan company

Visualization 1- Destibution of Borrowers APR¶

The Borrowers APR seem to be multimodal with a high spike around 0.37. The values typically ranges from o.05-0.43 which is a percentage of the annual rate charged

(Visualization 2- Destribution of Prosper score)¶

The Prospers score is a custom risk score build using historical prosper data. It ranges from 1-11 with 11 being the best or lowest risk socre. The most common scores are 4,6,and 8 and the least common is 1

(Visualization 3- Destribution of loan amount)¶

The loan original amount is destributd between 10000- 35000. Most of the values fall in the lower half of the amount. There also is spike in multiples of 5000, indicating most people borrow in multiples of 5000

Borrowers APR vs. Prosper score¶

There is a negative corrolation between the prosper score and the APR. Borrowers with better scores have lesser APR

Borrowers APR vs. Loan Amount¶

There is also a negative reationship between the loan amount and the APR. Hence The more the loan amount the lower the APR. this could indicate a strategy to encourage borrowers to borrow more.
